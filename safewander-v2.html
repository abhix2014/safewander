8 Days Solo in Rishikesh — A Complete Backpacker's Log

Nov 5 · Day 1
📍 Rishikesh Bus Stand → Laxman Jhula
Arrived by overnight bus at 5am. The air smells like incense and river. Dumped bags at a hostel I found via SafeWander — Ganga View — and was immediately chatting with 3 other solo travelers staying there. First rule of Rishikesh: the community finds you before you find it. Walked to Laxman Jhula at sunrise. Completely deserted. Just me, the bridge swaying, and the Ganga below.
🌅 Arrival 🌉 Laxman Jhula 💰 ₹350 spent
Nov 6 · Day 2
📍 Shivpuri Rafting Launch Point
Joined a SafeWander group rafting session — 6 of us, all background-verified. The guide was incredible. Grade 3 rapids were manageable even for beginners. The stretch near Marine Drive had a Grade 4 section that threw everyone around. Laughed until I cried. Lunch on a sandbar with the whole group. This is what solo travel is about.
🌊 Rafting 👥 Group of 6 💰 ₹800 spent
Nov 7 · Day 3
📍 Parmarth Niketan Ashram
5am wake-up for yoga. Worth every second of sleep lost. The class overlooks the Ganga and as the sun rose behind the mountains, I completely understood why people come to Rishikesh for spiritual journeys. Evening Ganga Aarti at Parmarth was overwhelming — hundreds of diyas floating on the river. Absolute silence in my mind for the first time in months.
🧘 Yoga 🔥 Aarti 💰 ₹250 spent
Nov 8 · Day 4
📍 Neelkanth Mahadev Trek
22km trek to Neelkanth Mahadev. Started at 6am with 2 people I matched with on SafeWander. The forest trail is dense and the incline is serious — took 6 hours up and down. Temple at the top is stunning. Met a group of pilgrims who shared prasad with us. My legs gave up on the descent but the view from the top made it completely worth it.
🏔 Trek 22km 💰 ₹150 spent
Nov 9 · Day 5
📍 Beatles Ashram
Rest day. Wandered into the Beatles Ashram in the afternoon — it's haunting and beautiful. Abandoned buildings covered in graffiti art. Stayed there for 3 hours sketching and reading. Found a hidden rooftop lookout nobody mentions. The view of the Ganga bending below is the most photographed non-photo in my collection.
🧘 Heritage 📷 Photography 💰 ₹100 spent
Nov 10 · Day 6
📍 Triveni Ghat & Lakshman Jhula Market
Full morning at Triveni Ghat watching the sunrise rituals. The market behind Laxman Jhula is chaotic and wonderful. Bought a Himalayan singing bowl from an old man who played it for 10 minutes to demonstrate. Warning from a fellow SafeWander member about petty theft near the market after 8pm — kept bags close, no issues.
🔥 Triveni Ghat 🛍 Market 💰 ₹1,200 spent
Nov 11 · Day 7
📍 Camping at Shivpuri Beach
Booked a riverside camp through the SafeWander hostel directory. 8 of us strangers around a bonfire on a Ganga sandbar. Someone had a guitar. The mountains were dark silhouettes against a sky full of stars. No one checked their phone. I'll remember this night for the rest of my life.
🏕 Camping 🔥 Bonfire 💰 ₹350 spent
Nov 12 · Day 8
📍 Rishikesh Bus Stand · Departure
Last morning chai at a rooftop café watching the Ganga. Said goodbye to 7 people who I met as strangers and leaving as friends. SafeWander made every connection feel safe and intentional. This isn't the end — I have a Kedarnath trek planned for February with 3 people I met here. Rishikesh doesn't let you leave; it just gives you a reason to return.
🎒 Departure 🔁 Will return 💰 Total: ₹9,800
